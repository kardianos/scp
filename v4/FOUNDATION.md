# V4 FOUNDATION — Self-Trapped Propagating Waves

## §1 — Core Thesis

Particles are self-trapped electromagnetic waves. The fundamental field is a nonlinear wave field — electromagnetic in character — that admits two classes of solution:

1. **Open waves** (linear regime): freely propagating EM radiation. These are photons.
2. **Enclosed waves** (nonlinear regime): self-trapped, oscillating, localized wave packets. The wave's own energy density modifies local propagation speed, creating a potential well that confines the wave. These are particles.

The distinction between light and matter is dynamical, not ontological: matter is light that has trapped itself through nonlinear self-interaction. E = mc² is literal — mass is confined wave energy.

## §2 — Physical Picture

### 2.1 Open vs Enclosed Waves

An electromagnetic wave propagates as an oscillating field pattern. It is "open" — wave fronts expand, energy disperses, and we observe wave-particle duality because the wave nature manifests in propagation (interference, diffraction) while the particle nature manifests in detection (localized energy absorption).

Now consider the same wave, but strong enough that its own energy density modifies the local propagation speed. Where the field is intense, waves propagate slower (effective refractive index n > 1). This self-induced slow region acts as a potential well. If the well is deep enough, the wave cannot escape — it oscillates back and forth within the well it created.

The result is a **self-trapped wave**: a localized, oscillating field configuration that sustains itself indefinitely. This is a particle.

### 2.2 Two-Ended Structure

The self-trapped wave oscillates along an internal axis — a standing wave in a cavity made by the wave itself. The two "ends" are the antinodes of the standing wave. For a vector field (like EM), the field vector points one way at one end and the opposite way at the other: a natural dipole.

This is:
- **NOT** a topological knot (no winding number)
- **NOT** a static soliton (the field oscillates internally)
- **NOT** a point particle (extended wave pattern with characteristic size)

### 2.3 Propagation vs Travel

A wave does not travel — it propagates. The field is everywhere; the wave is a disturbance pattern moving through it. When a particle "moves," the self-trapped wave pattern shifts position, like a pulse on a string. Nothing material moves; the pattern propagates.

This resolves wave-particle duality: a particle IS a wave (self-trapped), so it has wave and particle properties simultaneously. The apparent duality is an artifact of treating open and enclosed waves as different things.

### 2.4 Why EMF Shows Duality

An open EM wave is not enclosed — its energy is not confined. It propagates freely and can interfere with itself across extended regions (wave behavior) while delivering energy in localized quanta (particle behavior). The duality is real because an open wave genuinely has both extended (wave) and localized (quantum) character.

An enclosed wave resolves this: the wave IS localized, so "particle" and "wave" are the same object. No duality — just a self-trapped wave.

## §3 — Self-Trapping Mechanism

### 3.1 The Self-Consistent Potential Well

Self-trapping works through a feedback loop:

1. Strong field amplitude → high energy density ε(x)
2. High ε → reduced local phase velocity v_ph < c
3. Reduced v_ph → effective potential well: Φ_eff = ½c²(1 - v²_ph/c²)
4. Potential well → supports a bound eigenmode of the wave field
5. The bound eigenmode → concentrated field → strong amplitude → (return to 1)

The self-consistent condition: the field configuration that creates the potential well must be exactly the bound state supported by that well. This is a **nonlinear eigenvalue problem**.

### 3.2 BLV Effective Metric (from v2/v3)

The phase velocity in a nonlinear field theory on a background Ψ₀:

    v²_ph(x) = c² · P(x) / m(x)

where P(x) = effective elasticity, m(x) = effective inertia, both depending on the background field strength. In v2/v3, this was computed on a static Skyrmion background (external input). In v4, the background IS the self-trapped wave — no external structure needed.

### 3.3 The Mass Gap Requirement

For a self-trapped solution to exist, the oscillation frequency ω must satisfy:

    ω² < m²_eff(core)

where m²_eff is the effective mass² generated by the nonlinearity at the core center. At spatial infinity, m²_eff → m²_free (the free-field mass).

- **Massive field** (m_free > 0): Bound states exist for ω < m_free. The solution decays as exp(-κr) with κ = √(m²_free - ω²). These are Q-balls.

- **Massless field** (m_free = 0, like EM): Classical Q-balls do not exist. Self-trapping requires the nonlinearity to generate an effective mass gap DYNAMICALLY. The nonlinearity must create a region where the field is effectively massive, surrounded by a massless exterior. This is **dynamical mass generation** — the central challenge.

### 3.4 Saturating Nonlinearity

To prevent collapse (the wave focusing to a point), the nonlinearity must saturate at high field strength. The Born-Infeld Lagrangian provides exactly this: field strength is bounded by the parameter b. At low amplitude, Maxwell. At high amplitude, saturation prevents blow-up. This is the relativistic analog of saturable self-focusing in nonlinear optics.

## §4 — Candidate Lagrangians

### 4.1 Born-Infeld Electrodynamics (Primary Candidate)

The Lagrangian:

    L_BI = -b² [ √(1 + F²/(2b²) - (F·F̃)²/(16b⁴)) - 1 ]

where F² = F_μν F^μν, F·F̃ = F_μν F̃^μν, b = maximum field strength.

| Property | Status |
|----------|--------|
| Linear limit | L → -¼F² (Maxwell) |
| Lorentz invariant | Yes |
| Gauge invariant | Yes |
| Saturating | Yes (|F| ≤ b) |
| Static solitons | Yes (BIon: finite-energy point charge) |
| Oscillating self-trapped solutions | **Unknown** — this is the key question |

**Why BI is the primary candidate**: It IS electrodynamics with self-interaction. Open waves are literal photons. If enclosed waves exist, they are literal self-trapped EM radiation. The BIon already shows that BI regularizes the Coulomb self-energy (finite mass for a point charge). The question is whether the same nonlinearity supports oscillating bound states.

### 4.2 DBI (Born-Infeld + Dilaton)

    L_DBI = -b² [ √(1 + (∂σ)²/b² + F²/(2b²)) - 1 ]

Adds a real scalar σ (dilaton). The scalar provides:
- A monopole-coupled channel (needed for 1/r gravity — the v3 lesson)
- An additional DOF that participates in self-trapping
- Coupling to the BI field through the determinant structure

The dilaton is massless and couples universally to the energy density → leading gravity candidate.

### 4.3 Complex Proca + Quartic (Prototype)

    L_CP = -¼|F_μν|² + ½m²|W_μ|² - ¼λ(|W_μ|²)²

Complex massive vector field. Properties:
- Vector Q-ball solutions (proven to exist)
- Massive → exponentially localized
- U(1) charge conservation → stability
- Dipolar internal structure (vector → "two-ended")
- Breaks gauge invariance (Proca mass term)

This is the simplest model that definitively has the solutions we want. Useful as a proof-of-concept and for developing numerical methods before attacking the harder massless theories.

### 4.4 Scalar Q-Ball (Simplest Prototype)

    L_S = ½|∂φ|² - V(|φ|²)

with V(s) = ½m²s - ¼λs² (self-focusing potential). Complex scalar field.

- Q-ball solutions: φ = f(r) e^{iωt}, ω ∈ (0, m)
- Spherically symmetric (no dipolar structure)
- Well-studied, proven existence and stability results
- Excellent testbed for numerical methods

## §5 — Solution Taxonomy

### 5.1 Open Waves (Linear Regime)

Standard Maxwell plane waves at |F| ≪ b:

    E = E₀ cos(k·x - ωt),  B = k̂ × E/c,  ω = c|k|

Massless, dispersionless, polarized. These are photons.

### 5.2 Q-Balls (Massive Theories with Conserved Charge)

For the complex scalar with mass m and self-focusing potential:

    φ(x,t) = f(r) e^{iωt},  ω ∈ (0, m)

Profile ODE:

    f'' + (2/r)f' + (ω² - m²)f + λf³ = 0
    f'(0) = 0,  f(∞) = 0

Mass: M = 4π ∫₀^∞ [½(f')² + ½(m² + ω²)f² - ¼λf⁴] r² dr
Charge: Q = 4πω ∫₀^∞ f² r² dr
Stability: E(Q) < mQ (energetically favorable vs Q free particles)

### 5.3 Vector Q-Balls (Proca)

For the complex vector field W_μ with z-polarized ansatz:

    W = (0, 0, 0, w(r,θ) e^{iωt})

giving an axially symmetric, dipolar field pattern. The profile w(r,θ) satisfies a 2D elliptic PDE. The solution has maximal amplitude along the z-axis with two lobes (θ = 0, π) — the "two ends."

### 5.4 Born-Infeld Oscillons (Hypothetical)

If BI supports self-trapped oscillating solutions, the ansatz would be:

    A_μ(x,t) = a_μ(r, θ) cos(ωt)  (simplest: linearly oscillating)

or more generally a quasi-periodic oscillation. The BI nonlinearity generates an effective mass gap at high amplitude, potentially trapping the oscillation. The oscillation frequency ω would be determined self-consistently.

**This is the central existence question of the v4 program.**

## §6 — Particle Properties from Wave Structure

### 6.1 Mass

    M = E/c² = (1/c²) ∫ T₀₀ d³x

Mass is confined wave energy. E = mc² is the definition: mass IS trapped radiation energy divided by c². For a Q-ball of frequency ω, M(ω) is a computable function — the "mass spectrum."

### 6.2 Electric Charge

    Q = ∫ j⁰ d³x

For U(1)-symmetric theories, charge comes from the internal phase rotation:

    Q = ω ∫ |φ|² d³x  (scalar)
    Q = ω ∫ |W|² d³x  (vector)

**Charge quantization**: Not automatic in classical theory. Requires quantum mechanics (Bohr-Sommerfeld: Q = 2πn) or topological mechanism (Dirac quantization from monopoles). This is an open problem (§10, OP2).

### 6.3 Spin

| Field type | Spin | Internal structure |
|-----------|------|-------------------|
| Scalar Q-ball | 0 | Spherical, pulsating |
| Vector Q-ball (linear polarization) | 1 | Dipolar, oscillating along axis |
| Vector Q-ball (circular polarization) | 1 | Rotating internal field |
| Spinor (hypothetical) | ½ | Helical internal circulation |

Spin-½ requires a spinor-valued field. Whether this can arise from a purely vector (EM-like) framework, or requires the Clifford algebra structure from v2/v3, is an open problem (§10, OP3).

### 6.4 Size and Form Factors

The charge radius:

    ⟨r²⟩ = ∫ ρ(r) r² d³x / ∫ ρ(r) d³x

where ρ(r) is the energy density (mass form factor) or charge density (charge form factor).

The electromagnetic form factors F₁(q²), F₂(q²) are Fourier transforms of the density profiles, exactly as computed in v3 for the Skyrmion.

## §7 — Inter-Particle Forces

### 7.1 Linear Wave Exchange (EM Force)

Two self-trapped waves interact through their far-field tails. If the underlying field is massless (EM), the static tail is Coulomb:

    V_EM(R) = Q₁Q₂ / (4πR)

The "photon" mediating this force is a linearized fluctuation of the SAME field that constitutes the particles. Both the messenger and the matter are excitations of one field — light mediates forces between trapped light.

### 7.2 Nonlinear Overlap (Short-Range / Nuclear)

When two self-trapped waves are close enough for core overlap, the nonlinear self-interaction produces a short-range force. The range is set by the solution's characteristic size R₀:

    V_NL(R) ~ exp(-R/R₀) · f(orientation)  (if massive)
    V_NL(R) ~ 1/R^n · g(orientation)        (if massless tail)

This is the analog of nuclear forces. In the Skyrme model, this was the dipole-dipole 1/R³ interaction (v3 depletion result).

### 7.3 Gravity (Open Problem)

**The v3 lesson**: 1/r gravity requires a MASSLESS field with MONOPOLE (L=0) coupling to T₀₀. No Skyrme model channel satisfied both.

**What changes in v4**:

1. **Massless field**: The BI field is inherently massless. Open waves propagate at c. The far-field tail of a BIon is Coulomb (1/r), not Yukawa.

2. **Monopole coupling**: The BIon has a monopole (charge) source. The question is whether the ENERGY density (not just charge) creates a monopole field that other particles feel.

3. **Dilaton channel (DBI)**: If a scalar dilaton σ exists and is massless, its coupling to T₀₀ is automatic and monopole. This gives:

        V_grav(R) = -G_eff M₁ M₂ / R

   with G_eff = g²_σ/(4π) where g_σ is the dilaton-energy coupling. The question is whether G_eff matches Newton's G.

4. **Oscillating source**: Unlike a static Skyrmion, the self-trapped wave oscillates at frequency ω. The time-averaged T₀₀ is static (monopole), but the oscillating part radiates. The radiated energy carries momentum → radiation reaction → effective force between oscillating sources. This is a new channel not present in v2/v3.

### Phase 3: Born-Infeld Oscillons — THE KEY PHASE

**Complexity**: High. **Purpose**: Does BI self-trap?

**Model**: L_BI = -b²(√(1 + F²/(2b²)) - 1)

**Tasks**:
1. **Effective mass analysis**: Linearize BI around a strong background F₀. Compute effective mass² for perturbations δF. If m²_eff(F₀) > 0, self-trapping is possible.
2. **1+1D reduction**: Spherical symmetry + electric field only: A₀ = a(r,t). Derive the radial PDE. Search for time-periodic solutions (Floquet analysis or direct time evolution).
3. **Gaussian pulse evolution**: Initialize a localized EM pulse in 3+1D BI. Evolve numerically. Does it self-trap or disperse? Scan over pulse amplitude (ratio E₀/b) and width.
4. **Oscillon search**: If Gaussian disperses, try shaped initial data (e.g., BIon profile modulated by cos(ωt)). Scan over ω.
5. **If POSITIVE** (self-trapping found):
   - Characterize: measure ω, lifetime, energy, size
   - Check structure: is it dipolar? spherical? other?
   - Stability: perturb and evolve
   - This is the central result of v4
6. **If NEGATIVE** (no self-trapping in pure BI):
   - Diagnose: why? (likely: no effective mass gap for massless field)
   - Proceed to DBI: add dilaton, repeat search
   - The dilaton provides an extra DOF that can supply the mass gap

**Output**: Definitive answer on BI self-trapping. If negative, diagnosis + DBI extension.

## §10 — Open Problems

### OP1: Born-Infeld Self-Trapping (Phase 3)

Does pure BI support oscillating self-trapped solutions in 3+1D? This is the existence question that determines whether the primary candidate works. No prior literature establishes this.

**If YES**: The program proceeds on its primary path.
**If NO**: Pivot to DBI (dilaton extension) or other nonlinear EM theories.

### OP2: Charge Quantization

Q-ball charge is continuous. Physical charges are quantized (e). Possible mechanisms:
- Quantum mechanics: charge quantization from single-valuedness of the wave function (Bohr-Sommerfeld quantization in the internal phase degree of freedom)
- Dirac quantization: if magnetic monopoles exist, electric charge is quantized
- Self-consistency: perhaps only discrete values of ω give stable solutions

### OP3: Spin-½

Classical vector fields yield integer spin only. Fermions (spin-½) require spinorial structure. Options:
- The Clifford algebra from v2/v3 re-enters as the spinor extension
- Spin-½ from topological defects in the vector field (skyrmion-in-phase-space)
- Fundamental spinor field added to the theory (Dirac + BI coupling)

This is a deep structural question. The v4 framework addresses bosons naturally; fermions require additional input.

### OP4: Stability Without Topology

Topological solitons (Skyrmions) are eternally stable in continuum theory. Self-trapped waves are stabilized by:
- Charge conservation (Q-balls): stable if E(Q) < mQ
- Dynamical trapping (oscillons): long-lived but may eventually radiate and decay

What is the lifetime of a BI oscillon? If finite, is it long enough (≫ proton lifetime ~10³⁴ years)?

### OP5: The Massless Self-Trapping Problem

For a massless field (pure BI, no Proca mass), the condition ω < m = 0 forbids standard Q-balls. Self-trapping must rely on DYNAMICAL mass generation: the nonlinearity creates an effective mass at high amplitude.

Key question: does the BI saturation (|F| ≤ b) generate a sufficient effective mass gap? Preliminary estimate: the BI effective equation for perturbations around a strong background F₀ ~ b has:

    m²_eff ~ b² · (background-dependent factor)

If m²_eff > 0, oscillating bound states can exist with ω < m_eff.

### OP6: Gravity (see §7.3 and Phase 5)

The v3 monopole coupling theorem constrains any gravity mechanism. The v4 framework must produce a massless, monopole-coupled channel. The DBI dilaton is the leading candidate, but pure BI may offer alternatives through its nonlinear structure.

### OP7: Quantum Field Theory

This framework is entirely classical. The correspondence with quantum mechanics:
- Wave function |ψ|² ~ field energy density profile (Born rule?)
- Uncertainty principle ~ Fourier relationship between position and momentum of the wave packet
- Measurement ~ nonlinear interaction that collapses the self-trapped wave

These are speculative connections that need rigorous development.

## §11 — Mathematical Details

### 11.1 Born-Infeld Equations of Motion

From L_BI, varying with respect to A_μ:

    ∂_ν [ (F^μν - (F·F̃)F̃^μν/(4b²)) / √(1 + F²/(2b²) - (F·F̃)²/(16b⁴)) ] = 0

In the weak-field limit: ∂_ν F^μν = 0 (Maxwell).

For spherical symmetry with E = E_r(r,t) r̂ only:

    ∂_t[E_r / √(1 - E²_r/b²)] = 0  (Gauss's law, no time dependence for monopole)
    ∂_r[r²E_r / √(1 - E²_r/b²)] = Q  (static BIon: E_r = Q/(4πr²√(1 + Q²/(4πbr²)²)))

For oscillating perturbations on a BIon background: linearize around the BIon and look for quasi-normal modes with Im(ω) = 0 (bound states) or Im(ω) < 0 (resonances).

### 11.2 Q-Ball Profile Equation (Scalar Prototype)

    f'' + (2/r)f' + (ω² - m²)f + g(f) = 0

where g(f) = -V'(f²)·f is the nonlinear source. Boundary conditions:

    f'(0) = 0  (regularity)
    f(∞) = 0   (localization)

Shooting parameter: f(0) = f₀. Eigenvalue: f₀(ω) such that f → 0 at infinity.

Energy and charge:

    E = 4π ∫₀^∞ [½f'² + ½ω²f² + V(f²)] r² dr
    Q = 4πω ∫₀^∞ f² r² dr

Stability condition: dQ/dω < 0 (Vakhitov-Kolokolov criterion for NLS-type equations).

### 11.3 Vector Q-Ball Ansatz

For the complex Proca field W_μ = (W₀, W_x, W_y, W_z) with axial symmetry:

    W₀ = 0,  W_x = 0,  W_y = 0,  W_z = w(ρ,z) e^{iωt}

where ρ = √(x² + y²). The field strength:

    F_{0z} = -iω w e^{iωt}
    F_{ρz} = -∂_ρ w · e^{iωt}

Energy density (time-averaged):

    ⟨T₀₀⟩ = ½ω²w² + ½(∂_ρw)² + ½(∂_z w)² + ½m²w² - ¼λw⁴

Profile PDE:

    ∂²w/∂ρ² + (1/ρ)∂w/∂ρ + ∂²w/∂z² + (ω² - m²)w + λw³ = 0

This is an axially symmetric nonlinear Helmholtz equation. The solution w(ρ,z) is maximal on the z-axis and decays in all directions. Along z, it has a dipolar profile (maximum at two symmetric points ±z₀).

### 11.4 Effective Mass in Born-Infeld

Consider BI with a strong background field E₀ ê_z. A small transverse perturbation δE ê_x satisfies:

    □δE_x + m²_eff(E₀) δE_x = 0

where:

    m²_eff(E₀) = 0   (for transverse perturbations in uniform background)

This vanishes because BI preserves gauge invariance → massless photons on ANY background.

**Implication**: Pure BI does NOT generate an effective mass gap for transverse modes. Self-trapping of transverse EM waves in pure BI is impossible by this argument.

**However**: Longitudinal/scalar modes, or modes in non-uniform backgrounds, may behave differently. And the DBI extension (with dilaton) does generate a mass gap. This is why Phase 3 must be done carefully, and why DBI is the backup.

### 11.5 DBI Effective Mass

For DBI with background dilaton σ₀(x):

    m²_eff(σ₀) = b² · ∂²V_σ/∂σ² |_{σ₀}

If V_σ = 0 (massless dilaton), the dilaton propagates at c everywhere → no self-trapping of the dilaton itself, but the dilaton can mediate between BI solitons.

If V_σ = ½μ²σ² (massive dilaton), the dilaton has range 1/μ → Yukawa. For 1/r gravity, need μ = 0.

The dilaton coupling to energy:

    g_σ = ∂M/∂σ₀ = -α ∫ T₀₀^{BI} d³x = -αM

where α is the dilaton-BI coupling constant. Gravity from dilaton exchange:

    V_grav(R) = -α²M₁M₂ / (4πR)

Comparison with Newton: G_N = α²/(4π) → α = √(4πG_N) ≈ 1.15 × 10⁻¹⁸ (in natural units).

## §13 — Caveats

### C1: No Topological Protection

Without winding numbers, solutions are stabilized dynamically (charge conservation, energetic favorability), not topologically. They may be metastable — long-lived but not eternal. Stability must be verified case-by-case.

### C2: BI Oscillons Are Unproven

No published reference establishes oscillating self-trapped solutions in pure Born-Infeld 3+1D. The §11.4 argument suggests they may not exist for transverse modes. Phase 3 is genuine research, not review.

### C3: Classical Theory Only

This framework is entirely classical. Quantum effects (tunneling, pair production, vacuum fluctuations) may change the picture qualitatively. The classical analysis establishes whether self-trapping works in principle.

### C4: Spin-½ Not Addressed

The framework naturally produces spin-0 and spin-1 particles. Fermions require additional structure (spinors, Clifford algebra, or other). This is a known gap.

### C5: Gravity Coupling Strength

Even if the dilaton provides 1/r gravity, the coupling α must be fine-tuned to match G_Newton. There is no a priori reason for α ≈ 10⁻¹⁸. This is the hierarchy problem in a new guise.
