## Open issues

### Major (would close major gaps in the framework)

1. **Why `g_Spin(7) = α^(1/4)`?**  The g_W² = 5·√α conjecture works
   numerically (0.28 % match) but has no Lagrangian derivation.  The
   factor √α suggests `g_parent² = √α`, but the mechanism that makes
   this the "natural" Spin(7) coupling is not identified.

2. **Why `(21/16)` for gravity?**  G_e = (21/16) · α²¹ matches at 0.25 %.
   The ratio is `dim Spin(7) / dim Cl(3,1)` (both v59-structural), but
   no Lagrangian mechanism mandates this specific combination.

3. **Why each Furey N takes its (Bit-L, Bit-F)?**  The Z₂ × Z₂ pattern
   `e_R = (1,0), d_R = (0,1), u_R = (1,1)` is observed but not derived.
   In particular, *why does the d-quark not couple to L (Spin(7) gauge content)?*

4. **What does the v58 fundamental equation specifically dictate?**  v58 has
   a multivector force law with `M ∈ Cl(3,1)`, density `ρ_M = ½ M M̃`, and
   chiral current.  Extending to the Furey ℂ⊗ℍ⊗𝕆 = 64-dim algebra and
   applying to all sectors hasn't been worked out concretely.

### Medium (would tighten the framework but not break it)

5. **The 0.3 % gaps** in g_W² and G_e are at the limit of running-coupling
   precision and quark-mass uncertainty.  Need either tighter empirical
   measurements (G_N has ~10⁻⁴ uncertainty currently) or a sub-leading
   correction to the conjectures.

6. **Quark Brannen phases** (φ_u ≈ −2.02 rad, φ_d ≈ +0.110 rad) remain
   empirical.  Our formula gives Q (the Koide invariant) but not the
   individual mass ratios.  Need a separate derivation for the phases.

7. **Neutrino sector (N=3)**: t²_N = 1 − 14/D_3 requires a structural
   D_3.  If the Z₂ × Z₂ pattern extrapolates to (0, 0), D_3 = 0 and the
   Brannen ansatz doesn't apply.  Consistent with neutrinos being
   non-Brannen, but not testable empirically given small mass uncertainties.

### Minor (technical gaps in Lean)

8. **General Killing-form formula proof**: We have `(N-2)/(n-2)` as a
   *definition* for `killingEmbeddingIndex`.  Deriving this from the actual
   Killing-form calculation requires substantial Lie-algebra formalisation
   in Mathlib (matrix Lie algebras, structure constants, trace identities).

9. **Brannen kernel matrix proof**: We prove the *algebraic identity*
   `Q = (1+2t²)/3` for amplitudes `s_k`.  The matrix interpretation
   (eigenvalues of `M = a(I + ξS + ξ̄ Sᵀ)`) is documented in comments but
   not formally derived in Lean.

### Speculative / longer-term

10. **Full SM gauge group emergence**: SU(3)_c comes from Furey's Cl(6)
    Witt decomposition (already in variant B).  SU(2)_L comes from the
    silent direction (this session's Lean theorem).  U(1)_Y is not yet

11. **Overall lepton mass scale `a`**: Predictions give Q (ratio) and

## Honest assessment

**What's solid:**
- The Lean infrastructure works and ~50 theorems are clean.
- The Cl(7)_even single-source identification is structurally sound.
- The numerical conjectures (g_W², G_e, quark Q) match at the 0.1–0.3 % level
  with v59-natural structural inputs.

**What's tentative:**
- The numerical matches don't prove the framework — they could be:
  (a) Real structural identities, OR
  (b) Coincidences at the 10⁻³ level (improbable but possible given several
      degrees of freedom in choosing structural numbers).
- The selection rule (which N → which Cl-grade) is not derived.

**What's missing (still):**
- A specific Lagrangian for the v59 framework.
- Quark mass *ratios* (not just Q).
- Neutrino masses.
- Full SM gauge sector (esp. U(1)_Y and Higgs).
- Quark CKM mixing.

**Net**: This session multiplied the v59 prediction tier by ~4× (added g_W,
G correction, quark Q) and added a clean single-source unification.  But
the *mechanism* — what Lagrangian or principle FORCES the framework — is
not yet identified.

The structure is suggestive enough to keep developing.  The skeptic's view
that "it's all numerology" is hardest to rule out at the 0.1 % level given
the empirical uncertainties.  Tighter tests would come from:
- More precise G_N measurement (currently ~10⁻⁴, target ~10⁻⁶).
- Quark mass-ratio measurements with running-scheme controls.
- An explicit Lagrangian whose predictions don't have free coefficient choice.
