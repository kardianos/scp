## 2. Rigorous Reconstruction of the Composite Picture

### 2.1 In the Furey Fock Space (Cl(6) ≅ ℂ ⊗ 𝕆 minimal ideals)

The one-generation fermion states arise as the minimal left/right ideals generated by the Fock vacuum |vac⟩ and the three color-creation operators α_i (i=0,1,2) satisfying the Clifford relations of Cl(6). Occupation number N (number of α applications) labels the Furey N quantum number:

- N=0: |vac⟩ → e_R (or appropriate chirality convention) — lepton color singlet  
- N=1: α_i |vac⟩ → d_R^r,g,b (color triplet)  
- N=2: α_j α_k |vac⟩ → u_R (antitriplet, on complementary color indices)  
- N=3: α_0 α_1 α_2 |vac⟩ → ν_R / positron-like lepton  

(See [`../furey_construction/07_full_generation.py`](../furey_construction/07_full_generation.py) lines 80–89 for the explicit state construction and [`02_sm_idempotent.py`](../furey_construction/02_sm_idempotent.py) for the underlying Witt decomposition.)

The N=2 u-state is *literally* the exterior/wedge product of two N=1 d-creations (on the two colors not chosen for the first α). In the Clifford algebra language, even-grade operators (which populate the even subalgebra Cl(7)_even in which the Brannen kernels live) acting on a double-creation state necessarily sample *both* the Lie-algebra grades (Λ² ⊕ Λ⁶ = L) and the form grades (Λ⁴ = F) that appear when the two odd generators are multiplied. The Z₂ × Z₂ bit assignment is the direct record of this algebraic stacking: the double creation “sees” both structural pieces of the parent algebra.

Thus the u-quark representation (63-dimensional ambient) is *induced* from the lepton (L) and d-quark (F) pieces; there is no independent 63-dimensional u-representation that must be postulated separately. The u-quark is the composite object in the ideal decomposition.

## 3. Mass Emergence: Functional Relations

The Brannen scale a and the constraint |ξ|² together determine the mass eigenvalues via the three amplitudes a(1 + 2 t cos(2π k/3 + φ)). The apex (heaviest) mass in a sector is approximately a² (1 + 2 t)².

### 3.1 The 72 and 35 Factors

Empirically:

- a_u² / a_ℓ² ≈ 72.51  
- a_u² / a_d² ≈ 35.017 (matches D_F = 35 to 0.05 % within quark-mass systematics)

From the y_top ≈ 1 identity (m_t ≈ v / √2 with v = 28² a_ℓ²) and the composite-derived u-apex factor (1 + 2 √(7/9))² ≈ 7.640 one obtains exactly

a_u² / a_ℓ² = 28² / (√2 · (1 + 2 √(7/9))²) ≈ 72.57

which reproduces the observed 72.51. The 35 factor is literally D_F itself. Thus both cross-sector scale relations are *induced* once the composite origin of D_u (and therefore of t_u² = 7/9) is accepted. The u-sector Brannen parameters are not inserted independently; they follow from the lepton scale bridge + the composite ambient + the requirement y_top ≈ 1.

### 3.2 Kernel-Level Composition Tests

Because all three sectors share the *same* cyclic generator S on generation space, the Brannen kernels M_ℓ and M_d are elements of the same 3-dimensional algebra ℂ[S] / (S³ − 1). Their product (or any ring operation) is again an element of that algebra and therefore another Brannen kernel M' = a' (I + ξ' S + ξ̄' S²).

Explicit computation (with structural t_ℓ = √(1/2), φ_ℓ = 2/9, t_d = √(3/5), φ_d = 0 or empirical 0.11, a = 1) yields an effective t' ≈ 0.947–0.966, *not* exactly √(7/9) ≈ 0.8819. The sum M_ℓ + M_d likewise fails to reproduce 7/9.

**Null result that clarifies the theory**: the composite mechanism is *not* a simple matrix product (or sum) in flavor space. It operates at the level of the *internal grade embedding* of ξ itself (which ℍ-slice inside which grades of Cl(7)_even). The flavor algebra is common to all sectors; the distinction between lepton, d-quark, and u-quark is which grades of the single-source algebra are activated by the corresponding ξ. This is consistent with the Fock-space and Z₂ × Z₂ pictures above.
