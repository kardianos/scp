## The Kernel

In Cl(3,1) with signature (+,+,+,−), define the symmetric spatial bivector

$$B \;=\; \frac{e_{12} + e_{23} + e_{31}}{\sqrt{3}}, \qquad B^2 = -1$$

and the Z₃ rotor

$$R \;=\; \exp(B\,\pi/3) \;=\; \cos(\pi/3) + B\sin(\pi/3) \;=\; \tfrac{1}{2} + \tfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\,B$$

with $R^3 = -1$ (spinor double cover) and $RR^\dagger = 1$. R rotates spatial vectors cyclically: $e_1 \to e_2 \to e_3 \to e_1$.

On the 3-dim vector subspace, R acts as the cyclic shift matrix S. The lepton mass operator is

$$M_\ell \;=\; a\,\big(I \;+\; \xi\,S \;+\; \xi^*\,S^\dagger\big)$$

with $\xi = |\xi|\,e^{B\varphi}$ — complex because $B^2 = -1$ lets B play the role of $i$. Hermiticity is automatic.

## Eigenvalues — Brannen by Construction

Analytic eigenvalues:

$$\lambda_k \;=\; a\,\big(1 + 2|\xi|\cos(\varphi + 2\pi k/3)\big), \qquad k = 0, 1, 2$$

This is *exactly* Brannen form. Verified numerically: analytical eigenvalues match `numpy.linalg.eigvals` to 1.3 × 10⁻¹⁵.

The structural identities:
- $\sum_k \lambda_k = 3a$ (cosines at 120° spacing sum to 0)
- $\sum_k \lambda_k^2 = a^2(3 + 6|\xi|^2)$
- Koide ratio: $Q = \dfrac{\sum \lambda^2}{(\sum \lambda)^2} = \dfrac{1}{3} + \dfrac{2|\xi|^2}{3}$

So **$|\xi|^2 = \frac{3Q - 1}{2}$**, and $Q = 2/3$ iff $|\xi|^2 = 1/2$.

## What the Algebra Gives For Free

1. **Brannen form of the eigenvalues**: any Z₃-cyclic Hermitian operator on the 3-vector subspace of Cl(3,1) has eigenvalues of the form $a(1 + 2|\xi|\cos(\varphi + 2\pi k/3))$. No fitting — the form is structural.

2. **The "imaginary unit" comes from the algebra**: the bivector B squares to −1, so $\exp(B\varphi)$ is well-defined inside Cl(3,1). The phase φ is a real number; complex structure is generated by the algebra, not imposed externally.

3. **One degree of freedom eliminated**: 3 measured masses → 2 free parameters. The Koide identity $Q = 2/3$ is no longer a fit; it is a *consequence* of $|\xi|^2 = 1/2$. The cyclic symmetry IS the structural reason for the form.

## What the Algebra Does NOT Give

Two independent empirical residues remain:

### Residue 1: |ξ|² = 1/2

The Z₃-symmetric kernel admits any $|\xi| \ge 0$ — the magnitude is a free parameter of the operator. The Koide condition pins it to $1/\sqrt{2}$, but nothing in Cl(3,1) by itself forces this value.

Candidate dynamical sources:

- **Potential**: $V(|\xi|^2) = \lambda (|\xi|^2 - 1/2)^2$. Simple, but requires an explanation for *why* the minimum sits at 1/2 specifically.
- **Unitarity inside a larger object**: if $M_\ell$ is the restriction of a unitary operator on a larger algebra, $|\xi|^2 = 1/2$ might arise as a unitarity constraint. Need to identify the larger object.
- **Topological / homotopy**: if $\xi$ is the holonomy of a connection around a non-trivial cycle, |ξ| could be pinned by topology.
- **Anomaly inflow**: $\xi$ might be a Wess-Zumino coefficient whose magnitude is fixed by a discrete anomaly.

### Residue 2: φ = 2/9 rad

The phase $\varphi$ is similarly a free parameter. The empirical value is consistent with exactly 2/9 rad within m_τ uncertainty (Δ ≈ 7.4 × 10⁻⁶), but as the 2/9-rad audit (experiments 02–03) showed, 2/9 rad does not appear naturally in standard geometric, algebraic, modular, or hyperbolic structures.

The kernel doesn't fix it. Possible dynamical sources:

- Berry phase on a non-trivial bundle.
- RG fixed point on the moduli space of φ.
- Vacuum-angle quantization in a theory with discrete shift symmetry.
