## Key Findings

### 1. UDD chirality is the strongest 3D composite (X3c, S=1.23)

The "neutron-like" UDD configuration ‚Äî one Up-chirality braid + two Down-chirality
braids arranged along the three spatial axes ‚Äî produced the highest score in Gen 2
and the first genuinely 3D composite to show strong binding.

**Chirality comparison at A=0.4:**

| Chirality | S_final | E_pot | Interpretation |
|-----------|---------|-------|----------------|
| UUU | 0.535 | -45 | All-same: destructive symmetric interference |
| UUD | 0.816 | -82 | Proton-like: moderate asymmetric binding |
| UDD | **1.233** | **-136** | Neutron-like: strongest asymmetric binding |

The progression UUU ‚Üí UUD ‚Üí UDD shows that **more chirality asymmetry creates
stronger binding**. The fully symmetric UUU is worst because all three braids
interfere identically ‚Äî there's no preferred triple-product coupling direction.
UDD has the most asymmetry and the strongest constructive V(P) coupling.

### 2. Higher UUD amplitude helps (X3d, S=0.96)

X3d (UUD at A=0.5) scored 0.96 vs X3b (UUD at A=0.4) at 0.82. Amplitude
scaling improves 3-braid composites just as it did for single braids.
**Prediction**: UDD at A=0.5 would likely exceed X3c's score.

### 3. Amplitude scaling from evolved braids saturates

Tracks 1-3 (modifying the T=200 baseline braid SFA) clustered around S=0.70-0.82.
The baseline braid has already partially radiated by T=200, so scaling its late-stage
state doesn't recover the initial binding strength. Starting fresh (as the 3-braid
seeds do) gives higher scores because the initial field amplitudes are at their peak.

### 4. Very high ellipticity collapses (C7b, S=0.47)

Ellipticity of 0.65 is too extreme ‚Äî the braid cross-section is so flattened that
the traveling wave can't self-reconstruct. The optimal range appears to be 0.3-0.5.

### 5. Counter-braid tightening weakens binding (C3b, S=0.71)

Bringing the counter-braid closer (shift 3 vs 5) reduced binding, likely because
the two counter-rotating waves destructively interfere at close range. There's an
optimal separation (~5 code units) where the V(P) coupling is constructive.

## Post-Gen2 Spatial Analysis

Detailed spatial analysis of 6 user-selected candidates, examining cluster
structure, theta stability, centroid drift, and boundary proximity.

### Q1: Common Stability Structures?

Two distinct structural patterns emerged:

**A. Multi-cluster ‚Üí merging (3-braid configs: X3c, X3b)**
- Start with 7 clusters (three braids + interference fringes)
- Clusters gradually merge as the structure self-organizes
- X3c: 7‚Üí7‚Üí10‚Üí7‚Üí4‚Üí4 (merging trend, 4 clusters at T=20)
- X3b: 7‚Üí7‚Üí7‚Üí9‚Üí3‚Üí3 (merging, 3 clusters at T=20)
- The initial fragmentation is the three braids plus their overlap regions

**B. Single-blob oscillation (amplitude-scaled: C4a, C4b, C4c)**
- Maintain 1 cluster throughout (occasionally split to 2-3, then reunify)
- C4a: 1‚Üí1‚Üí2‚Üí1‚Üí1 (very stable single blob)
- C4b: 1‚Üí1‚Üí3‚Üí1‚Üí1 (briefly fragments, recovers)
- These are essentially the baseline braid with scaled amplitude ‚Äî same topology

**C. Counter-braid consolidation (C3a)**
- Starts as 3 clusters, fragments to 6, then FULLY consolidates to 1 by T=20
- This is the most dramatic structural evolution ‚Äî two separate braids merging
  into a single coherent structure

### Boundary Proximity Warning

**R_rms values of 5‚Äì7 at L=15 are dangerously close to the absorbing boundary.**
The damping zone starts at r = L - damp_width = 12. Structures with R_rms > 5
have significant energy density extending to r = 10+ (within 2 code units of
the damping zone). This means:

- The absorbing BC is draining energy from the structure's outer halo
- Depletion profiles are truncated (can't measure long-range behavior)
- Binding energy measurements may be artificially reduced

**Recommendation**: Gen 3 should use L=25 with N=192 (similar dx=0.26 vs
current 0.24). Or L=30 N=256 on GPU for better margins.
