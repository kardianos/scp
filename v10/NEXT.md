**Status**: Not yet attempted. This is the "ultimate destination" per V9/NEXT.md.

**What it requires**:
1. Nonlinear EM Lagrangian (Born-Infeld or similar) — caps ρ_EM, preventing 1/A runaway
2. 3D or 2D-axisymmetric grid — topology (Hopfion knots) cannot exist in 1D
3. Topological stabilization — linked E/B field lines prevent partial-wave leaking

**Why it might work**:
- Born-Infeld: ρ_BI ~ b²√(1 + ρ_M/b²) → capped at field strength b
- The 1/A runaway requires ρ → ∞ as A → 0. BI prevents this.
- Combined with topology: partial waves can't leak independently (they're knotted)
- This breaks BOTH failure modes of Q9 simultaneously

**Why it's hard**:
- Jump from 1D ODE to 3D PDE (or 2D axisymmetric at minimum)
- Must debug metric, nonlinear EM, and topology simultaneously
- V9/NEXT.md recommends Phase 2 (flat-space hopfion) before Phase 3 (synthesis)

**Recommended approach** (per V9/NEXT.md Phase 2):

4. Evolve in time — does Q_H prevent partial-wave dispersal?

### Option C: Bag Model (The Boundary Fix)
**Status**: Not yet attempted.

**What it requires**:
1. Scalar confinement field φ(r) with broken-symmetry potential V(φ)
2. EM waves exert outward pressure on the bag wall
3. Bag energy contributes to total mass

**Why it might work**:
- Makes Rmax dynamical (self-consistent boundary)
- Bag energy density B contributes ~4/3 π R³ B to mass
- If B is tuned, could give correct M_p with stable Rmax

**Why it might not**:

- Bag model just makes Rmax self-consistent — doesn't resolve the tension
- Unless bag energy itself accounts for the "missing" mass

### Option D: Einstein-Dirac-Maxwell (EDM) Soliton
**Status**: Literature exists (Finster-Smoller-Yau 1999, Herdeiro et al. 2017+)

**What it requires**:
1. Replace EM scalar waves with Dirac spinor field
2. Fermion mass provides natural confinement scale
3. Self-consistent Einstein + Dirac + Maxwell system

**Why it's interesting**:
- Dirac field has intrinsic spin (no need for topology)
- Fermion mass prevents dispersal (ψ ~ e^{-mr}/r far from core)
- EDM solitons known to exist in literature (particlelike, finite mass)
- Closer to actual QCD (quarks are fermions, not scalars)

**Why it's different from V8-V10**:
- Not about trapping EM waves — about self-consistent fermion-EM-gravity bound states
- Dirac equation replaces wave equation (4-component spinor, not scalar)
- No box needed — fermion mass provides natural decay scale
