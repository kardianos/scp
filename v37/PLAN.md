# V37 Execution Plan: Compact Braid Geometry Search

## Candidates (ordered by simplicity)

### 1. Truncated Helix ("braid sausage")
Current helix with Gaussian z-envelope. Simplest test of compactness.

### 2. Borromean Rings
Three interlocking rings in orthogonal planes. Proven stable in V26.

### 3. Double Helix Closed Loop
Two half-helices joined at ends, bent into a circle. DNA-like ring.

### 4. Triple Closed-Loop Helix with Cross
Three helices, each closing on itself, interlinked/crossing at center.
Like three interlocked rubber bands, each one a helical loop, braided
through each other at a central crossing point. This creates a compact
knot with three-fold symmetry matching the three fields.
