## 1. The Dark Sector: Matter and Energy

### Dark Matter
CHPT's explanation for dark matter is the most straightforward of any alternative theory: **Dark matter is just normal matter that happens to be uncharged.**

*   **Mechanism**: Stable knots that lack chirality (charge) and lack the specific harmonic resonance to couple to the electromagnetic field.
*   **Mass**: They have density $\rho > \rho_0$, so they gravitate.
*   **Interaction**: They interact only via gravity (depletion zones) and potentially weak/contact forces.
*   **Status**: This is a direct prediction of the knot spectrum. If stable non-chiral knots exist, they *are* dark matter.

### Dark Energy
CHPT struggles with Dark Energy (accelerated expansion).
*   **Vacuum Energy**: The theory defines $\rho_0$ as the zero-energy state. If the vacuum has no energy, a cosmological constant $\Lambda$ is unnatural.
*   **Expansion**: If "expansion" is just the dilution of the field density over time, there is no obvious driver for *acceleration*.
*   **Speculation**: Perhaps the field has a "surface tension" or negative pressure component at cosmological scales. This is undeveloped.

## 2. Black Holes: Horizons Without Singularities

General Relativity predicts singularities (infinite density) because it assumes matter collapses to a point. CHPT avoids this naturally.

*   **Mechanism**: Matter collapses. Density increases.
*   **Limit**: The field has a maximum possible density (saturation) or a nonlinear restoring force that halts collapse at a finite radius.
*   **Result**: A "Black Star" or "Gravastar."
    *   **External**: Metric is Schwarzschild. Event horizon exists (null-rotors cannot escape).
    *   **Internal**: Smooth, high-density fluid. No singularity. No information loss paradox (information is preserved in the internal state).

This is a superior physical picture to standard GR.
