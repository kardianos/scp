# Abstract

We outline a program to derive effective three-dimensional spatial structure, including local Euclidean geometry and an approximately constant speed of light, from a purely pre-geometric multivector field without presupposing any background manifold. The sole primitive is a multivector field $M$ whose density $\rho_M$ and protected chiral structure generate a connection $\Omega$ through the living candidate equation

$$
\langle D \Omega + \lambda \Omega^2 + \mu \langle \Omega, \Omega \rangle \rangle_{0,2}
= f_g(\rho_{\rm ambient}) \cdot J_\rho + f_{\rm em}(\rho_{\rm ambient}) \cdot J_\chi.
$$

Effective three-dimensional spatial structure is defined here as a derived, observer-dependent description that can arise when the compensating interplay between density gradients and protected chiral currents produces stable causal neighborhoods. We explore quantitative criteria under which the coarse-grained relations among these neighborhoods approximate those of a Riemannian 3-manifold (with controlled error) at scales relevant to internal observers. The living candidate equation is taken as the central dynamical law whose feedback mechanisms may select such structure. We provide exploratory criteria for emergence, grounded in existing 2D retarded numerics, and describe how the Lean 4 theorem prover (together with graph-based and algebraic proxies) can be used to maintain rigor. The document integrates ontological foundations, potential mechanisms, and a phased implementation roadmap.

# 1. Motivation and Intent

The preceding work stabilized a living candidate multivector equation on two-dimensional retarded lattices and obtained both extensive numerical validation and the first complete machine-checked retarded implication theorems on real exported data. Those results were obtained on a fixed computational lattice. While useful as a discovery and proof-of-concept tool, a fixed lattice reintroduces a background geometry at the modeling level — precisely the ontological error identified in the layer critique.

The present program therefore adopts a stricter requirement:

**No background manifold is assumed at the fundamental level.**  
Three-dimensional spatial structure, including its metric properties and causal light cones, must be shown to arise as a stable, emergent description for observers composed of the same field that generates it.

This document serves three coupled purposes:

1. **Theory**: State the ontological foundations and derive quantitative conditions under which an effective 3D geometry appears.
2. **Intent**: Make explicit the criteria by which we will judge success or failure of any proposed emergence mechanism.
3. **Implementation Plan**: Define a staged, tool-assisted roadmap (with Lean as a primary rigor instrument) that keeps the work from drifting into hand-waving.

# 2. Ontological Foundations

## 2.1 The Primitive Layer

The only fundamental entity is a multivector-valued field $M$ (or a still more primitive relational structure whose local excitations admit a multivector description). 

- There are no points, no distances, and no pre-existing differentiable manifold.
- "Location" and "separation" are defined solely by causal influence relations mediated by the retarded dynamics of the living candidate.
- The field $M$ carries both a scalar density $\rho_M = \frac{1}{2}(M\tilde M - v^2)$ and internal chiral (bivector) degrees of freedom.

All higher structure — including what appears to an internal observer as three-dimensional space — is derived from the configuration and dynamics of $M$.

## 2.2 The Living Candidate (Background-Free Reading)

The equation

$$
\langle D \Omega + \lambda \Omega^2 + \mu \langle \Omega, \Omega \rangle \rangle_{0,2}
= f_g(\rho_{\rm ambient}) \cdot J_\rho + f_{\rm em}(\rho_{\rm ambient}) \cdot J_\chi
$$

is re-interpreted as follows:

- $\Omega$ is the connection generated by the field at a given relational "region."
- The operator $D$ encodes retarded causal influence: only past causal antecedents (defined by the light-cone structure generated by previous values of $\Omega$) contribute.
- $J_\rho$ and $J_\chi$ are extracted from the local density gradient and protected chiral current of $M$.
- The ambient functions $f_g$ and $f_{\rm em}$ are themselves functionals of the retarded history of $\rho_M$.

No coordinate chart or metric is used to define $D$ or the notion of "ambient." These are defined relationally via the causal web generated by prior field configurations.

## 2.3 Internal Observers

Any "observer" or measuring apparatus is itself a stable, high-density, protected-chirality excitation of the same multivector field. Consequently:

- The geometry experienced by the observer is the causal structure that its own internal modes can detect.
- Apparent 3D Euclidean relations are those that are stable under the compensating dynamics of density and chirality for the class of excitations that constitute the observer.

This closes the loop: geometry is not "seen from outside"; it is the effective relational structure that the field maintains among its own persistent modes.

# 3. Criteria for the Emergence of Effective 3D Geometry

We define effective three-dimensional spatial structure by the existence of a coarse-graining map that extracts, from a field configuration, quantities satisfying the following conditions to within controlled error.

## 3.1 Local Causal Neighborhoods

At every relational region $x$, the connection $\Omega(x)$ defines a local light-cone structure: a set of directions in which null (maximally fast) propagation is possible. We require that these local cones be approximately round (i.e., the effective speed is nearly isotropic) when averaged over the protected chiral modes.

Analytic condition (motivated by 2D numerics):
$$
\left| \frac{c_{\rm eff}(\theta_1) - c_{\rm eff}(\theta_2)}{c_{\rm eff}} \right| < \epsilon_{\rm iso}
$$
for all pairs of directions $\theta_1, \theta_2$ within a protected excitation, where $\epsilon_{\rm iso} \approx 0.02$ is the isotropy tolerance already achieved in the 2D protected runs.

## 3.2 Effective Dimension

A network of causal neighborhoods must admit a coarse-graining in which the number of independent directions required to specify relative positions grows as $r^3$ (volume scaling) rather than $r^2$ or $r^1$. This can be quantified by the growth rate of the number of distinguishable causal paths of retarded time $\tau$:

$$
N(\tau) \propto \tau^{d_{\rm eff}}, \qquad d_{\rm eff} \approx 3 \pm \delta
$$

with $\delta < 0.1$ inside the safe band.

## 3.3 Effective Metric and Curvature

There must exist a derived tensor $g_{\rm eff}$ (extracted from integrated null propagation times or from the bivector part of $\Omega$) such that the effective distance between two regions satisfies the triangle inequality and yields a Riemannian metric whose sectional curvature is bounded:

$$
|R_{\rm eff}| < \Lambda
$$

where $\Lambda$ is set by the inverse square of the largest density gradient scale in the configuration. The 2D work already demonstrated that density gradients produce bivector connection components $\omega \propto \nabla\rho_M$ whose integrated effect reproduces inverse-square forces; the 3D extension must show that the same mechanism produces a consistent 3-metric.

## 3.4 Stability Under Compensating Dynamics

The configuration must be an approximate fixed point of the coupled density–chirality dynamics. Small perturbations in $\rho$ or in the protected bivector winding must relax back to the same effective geometry rather than drive large deviations in local $c$ or isotropy. This is the precise content of the "compensating field effects" that stabilize the appearance of rigid 3D space and constant local $c$.

Quantitative target (extrapolated from 2D protected reduction):
The cross-grade leakage into the effective null structure must remain below 3 % (the level already achieved numerically) when the chiral protection rule is active.

## 3.5 Exploratory Considerations on Dimensionality Selection

A natural and important objection arises at this point: in generic discrete or relational models, causal networks do not spontaneously select an effective dimensionality near three. Random graphs and evolving causal sets frequently percolate into high-dimensional or fully connected structures, or else fragment into lower-dimensional trees. The hope that the specific dynamics of the living candidate will favor $d_{\rm eff} \approx 3 \pm \delta$ therefore requires substantive justification rather than assumption.

We do not claim that the equation magically produces three dimensions. Instead, we treat the question as an open, exploratory target for the program. The living candidate itself supplies several mechanisms that could, in combination, act as a self-constraining attractor:

- The density-sourcing term $f_g(\rho_{\rm ambient}) \cdot J_\rho$ couples local density directly to the generation of the connection $\Omega$. High local density strengthens (or weakens, depending on the sign of the modulation) the effective causal influence, creating a global feedback that penalizes both under-dense regions (where causal structure becomes too sparse to support stable excitations) and over-dense regions (where gradients may drive fragmentation).

- The chiral current $J_\chi$ and the protected-bivector rule introduce an additional selection pressure. Protected internal winding allows excitations to maintain higher local density without leaking into unwanted grades. In a relational setting, this could favor causal networks whose local connectivity permits the formation of many independent but non-percolating "spatial" directions — roughly three — before further connectivity begins to increase energy cost or destabilize the protection.

- The quadratic self-interaction terms $\lambda \Omega^2 + \mu \langle \Omega, \Omega \rangle$, active inside the empirically identified safe band, supply a nonlinear saturation mechanism. These terms can be viewed as an effective potential that resists both excessive spreading (which would dilute density) and excessive clumping (which would destroy causal structure). In graph language, they may act as a penalty on both very high and very low local coordination numbers, potentially selecting an intermediate effective dimension that maximizes stable protected density.

From this perspective, the relevant question is not "why exactly three?" in a fundamental sense, but rather: **what effective dimensionality permits the highest stable density of protected chiral modes while preserving a consistent causal structure for internal observers?** In such a framing, three spatial dimensions (plus time) may emerge as the lowest-dimensional manifold in which long-range forces (gravity via density gradients, electromagnetism via chiral currents) can coexist with stable, high-density localized excitations without immediate percolation or collapse. This is consistent with the observation that our actual physical environment is not a pure Euclidean 3-space: curvature from gravity and long-range effects from electromagnetism already modify strict 3D behavior at multiple scales. The "3" we seek is therefore best understood as the dominant effective dimensionality for observers composed of the field itself, rather than a universal fixed point.

A parallel observation applies to Lorentz invariance. Because the fundamental propagation rule is strictly retarded and limited to the local causal structure generated by $\Omega$ at each relational region, any observer whose internal modes can only access information through the field will, by construction, experience local Lorentz invariance. Violations can appear globally or in regions of extreme density gradient, but these are expected features rather than bugs; they would be detectable in principle as departures from the safe-band regime.

These considerations remain speculative at present. The program’s task is to determine, through the staged relational models and Lean-assisted analysis outlined below, whether the living candidate can generate a dynamical preference for the dimensionality range that maximizes stable protected density. If it cannot, the criteria in Sections 3.1–3.4 will need revision; if it can, the equation itself will have supplied the explanation rather than an external fine-tuning requirement.

# 4. Role of the Living Candidate in Emergence

The quadratic self-interaction terms $\lambda \Omega^2 + \mu \langle\Omega,\Omega\rangle$ (controlled by the safe band) play a dual role:

- They generate the near-field $1/r^2$ force on timelike excitations (already proved in Lean for the 2D case).
- They also contribute to the stabilization of the local light-cone shape. The same suppression of unwanted cross-grade terms that reduces force pollution for protected lumps also reduces pollution of the null directions, thereby helping maintain the isotropy required for an effective Euclidean 3-space.

The ambient modulation $f_g(\rho)$ introduces a global feedback: regions of high density weaken the effective gravitational response while simultaneously altering the causal propagation speed for null modes. This feedback is a candidate mechanism for the self-regulation that keeps most of space looking approximately flat and $c$ approximately constant to internal observers.

# 5. Formal Tools: Lean and Related Methods for Maintaining Sanity

Because the target ontology is background-free and the emergence criteria are subtle, informal reasoning is insufficient. We adopt a multi-tool strategy with Lean 4 as the primary instrument for maintaining logical hygiene.

## 5.1 Abstract Layer in Lean (Ontology Without Coordinates)

Define an abstract type `CausalRegion` whose only primitive operations are:

- `causalPast : CausalRegion → Set CausalRegion` (retarded influence)
- `localOmega : CausalRegion → MV` (the multivector connection at that region)
- `density : CausalRegion → R`

All subsequent definitions (light cones, effective distance, effective dimension) are expressed in terms of these primitives. No `\(\mathbb{R}^3\)` or manifold type is introduced at this layer.

The living candidate is stated as an axiom relating `localOmega` to the sources extracted from `density` and protected chiral currents, using the same grade-projection structure already formalized in the existing `UnifiedMultivector` development.

## 5.2 Emergence Theorems

We aim to prove statements of the form:

**Theorem (Emergence of Effective 3-Metric).**  
Given a causal web satisfying:
- The living candidate pointwise,
- Density and chiral protection within the empirically determined safe band,
- Bounded gradient scale $\lvert\nabla\rho\rvert < \Gamma$,

there exists a map $\Phi$ from finite sets of causal regions to a Riemannian 3-manifold $(M,g)$ such that the pull-back of $g$ reproduces effective distances and light-cone angles to within $\epsilon$ (with explicit $\epsilon$ derived from the 2D numerical thresholds).

Lean is used to:
- Make the map $\Phi$ and the error bounds fully explicit.
- Discharge the algebraic steps (grade projections, quadratic suppression) that were already machine-checked in 2D.
- Track which numerical constants (e.g., the safe band bounds) remain as hypotheses versus which have been discharged on concrete finite models.

## 5.3 Finite Relational Models as Bridges

Because infinite or continuous background-free structures are difficult to handle directly, we construct finite graph models in which:

- Vertices carry multivector values.
- Edges are weighted by retarded causal strength (computed from the living candidate).
- No global coordinate system is imposed.

These graphs can be encoded directly in Lean (or in Python for rapid exploration, with selected subgraphs exported to Lean). We then ask Lean to verify that the extracted effective dimension, effective metric, and isotropy satisfy the criteria of Section 3 within the target tolerances.

This creates a tight feedback loop:
- Python generates large random or evolved graphs under the living candidate.
- Selected high-interest subgraphs are ingested into Lean.
- Lean certifies (or refutes) that the emergence criteria hold for that concrete realization.
- Failures drive refinement of the candidate or of the emergence map.

## 5.4 Additional Supporting Tools

- **Graph-theoretic dimension estimators** (box-counting, persistent homology on the causal graph) for quantitative $d_{\rm eff}$.
- **Category-theoretic language** (causal sets, toposes of sheaves over causal regions) for expressing observer-dependent coarse-grainings rigorously.
- **Symbolic/numeric Python prototypes** (using the existing `ga.py` library) to explore candidate emergence maps before they are formalized in Lean.

The division of labor is: Python for discovery and scale, Lean for proof and consistency, with explicit translation steps between them.

# 6. Staged Implementation Roadmap

**Phase 0 – Conceptual Stabilization (this document)**  
Refine the emergence criteria and the abstract Lean signature until both the theory team and the formalization team agree on the target statements.

**Phase 1 – Minimal Relational Models**  
Construct the smallest graphs (hundreds to a few thousand vertices) on which the living candidate can be evolved without a background lattice. Extract effective dimension and local light-cone statistics. Use Lean to certify the extraction algorithms.

**Phase 2 – Quantitative Emergence Maps**  
Define and implement (first in Python, then in Lean) one or more concrete maps from field configurations to effective 3-metrics. Measure error against the criteria of Section 3 on ensembles of graphs. Identify the minimal conditions on $\rho$, chiral protection, and $\Omega$ that suffice for $d_{\rm eff} \approx 3$ with controlled curvature.

**Phase 3 – Observer-Centric Coarse-Graining**  
Introduce explicit internal-observer models (stable high-density protected lumps with internal "clocks" and "rulers" built from the same multivector degrees of freedom). Demonstrate that the geometry they reconstruct from their own causal interactions matches the globally extracted effective metric to within tolerance.

**Phase 4 – Stability and Self-Regulation**  
Study the coupled density–chirality dynamics on relational graphs. Search for attractors in which the effective 3D geometry and local $c$ are self-stabilizing. Use Lean to prove invariance properties of these attractors under small perturbations (within the safe band).

**Phase 5 – Lifting to the Living Candidate**  
Return to the full living candidate equation and prove (in Lean, on finite models) that the quadratic and ambient-modulation terms are necessary and sufficient for the stability found in Phase 4. This closes the loop with the earlier 2D results.

Success metric for the entire program: the existence of at least one explicit, Lean-verified map from a background-free multivector configuration satisfying the living candidate to an effective Riemannian 3-manifold whose geometric quantities reproduce the classical limits already proved in 2D, with all error bounds stated and discharged.
