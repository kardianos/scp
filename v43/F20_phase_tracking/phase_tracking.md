# F20: Intermediate Phase Tracking in V42 Deuterium

## Data Source
- `/home/d/code/scp/v42/results/deuterium_output.sfa` (28 GB, N=512, L=100, 7 frames at t=0,100,200,300,400,500,500)
- Analysis tool: `phase_track.c` — BFS cluster detection + atan2 phase classification

## Method
Phase = atan2(phi_1, phi_0) at each active voxel (|P| > 0.002).
Three phase groups defined by +-0.5 rad windows:
- **Group A** (center -0.79 rad): dominant anti-phase group
- **Group B** (center +2.40 rad): secondary anti-phase group (A + pi)
- **Group C** (center +0.70 rad): **intermediate phase** (the "bond" candidate)

Baryon clusters identified by BFS on |P| > 0.01, sorted by integrated |P| mass.

**Phase group C is ABSENT for t=0 through t=400 and appears abruptly at t=500.**
It contains 16,927 voxels (1015 volume units), comparable in size to group A (18,155 voxels).

### Phase Histogram at t=500
The atan2 phase distribution at t=500 shows three distinct peaks:
- A peak at -0.79 rad (11,497 voxels, P_int=34.2)
- B peak at +2.36 rad (7,819 voxels, P_int=8.6)
- **C: a broad plateau from +0.26 to +1.31 rad** (peak at +0.79, 3780 voxels)
  Total C-region P_int = 3.26

The C peak is BROAD (spanning ~1 radian), unlike the sharp A and B peaks.

### Spatial Location of Phase C
At t=500:
- Baryon 1: (-22.5, 0.1, 2.5), mass=416.9
- Baryon 2: (-23.0, 1.3, 34.1), mass=134.8
- Bond midpoint: (-22.8, 0.7, 18.3), bond length=31.7
- **Phase C centroid: (-4.3, -1.2, 15.0)**
- Distance from C centroid to bond midpoint: **18.8** (59% of bond length)
- Fraction of C voxels within bond region (r < 0.5 * bond_len): **32.8%**

### Interpretation

**The intermediate phase is NOT concentrated between the two baryons.**

The phase-C centroid is displaced ~19 units from the bond midpoint, while the bond itself is only 32 units long. The C centroid is at x=-4.3, far from the baryon x-coordinates of -22.5 and -23.0. Only 33% of C voxels fall within the bond half-sphere.

This suggests the intermediate phase is:
1. **A diffuse background feature**, not a localized bond
2. Distributed broadly across the simulation volume, not concentrated on the bond axis
3. Likely generated by nonlinear wave interactions (phase mixing) at late times
4. Its broad spectral width (~1 rad) is consistent with incoherent radiation, not a phase-locked bond state

### Phase Evolution Summary
- t=0-200: System dominated by A-phase, B-phase is secondary. Pure two-phase oscillation.
- t=300: "Other" voxels appear (6724), indicating phase broadening begins. Histogram shows new peaks at -2.7 to -1.8 rad.
- t=400: B-phase balloons (45668 voxels), "other" persists (3692). Still no C-phase.
- t=500: **C-phase appears suddenly** (16927 voxels), while B-phase shrinks to 12263 and A stays at 18155. The "other" category also fills (3274).

The transition at t=500 appears to be a phase-space bifurcation where the B-phase population partially scatters into the C-phase window.

## Conclusion
The intermediate phase group at phi~0.7 rad does exist at t=500 as reported, but it is NOT a localized inter-baryon bond. It is a spatially diffuse, spectrally broad phase population, likely arising from nonlinear wave scattering. The nuclear bond hypothesis is **not supported** by this spatial analysis.

To test further: a simulation with finer time resolution (dt_save=10 instead of 100) near t=400-500 would clarify whether C appears gradually or suddenly, and whether it correlates with any topological event.
