#### 1. Analytical Tests
- **Nonlinear Dirac Equation**:
  - Limiting case: For g=0, reduces to standard Dirac (free particles, E = √(p² c² + m² c⁴)).
  - Soliton stability: For g<0 (attractive), stationary solutions exist as ψ(x) ~ sech(√(|g| m_0) x) in 1D approximations (transparent: Balance kinetic dispersion with nonlinear binding; energy minimization yields bound states).

  - Issue: Potential negative energies in some solutions, but literature shows stable positive-energy solitons in 3D.

- **Field Existence Equation**:
  - Newtonian limit: For weak fields (E ≈ E_v), ∇²E ≈ -ξ ρ; potential ϕ ≈ (c² / 2) log(E / E_v) ≈ - (ξ c² ρ / 2), set ξ = 8π G / c⁴ to match ∇²ϕ = 4π G ρ.
  - For point mass: E(r) = E_v - k / r (k= ξ M / (4π)); trough deepens near r=0, but V(ρ) repulsion prevents singularities.

- **Geodesic Law**:
  - Newtonian recovery: a = - (c² / 2) ∇ log E ≈ (c² / 2) (k / (E_v r²)) \hat{r} ≈ G M / r² \hat{r} (inward if signs set for attraction; transparent: ∇ log E = - (k / (E r²)) for E = E_v - k/r ≈ E_v).
  - Relativistic hint: Log form suggests conformal rescaling, akin to emergent metrics.

- **Density Halo Equation**:
  - Limit: As E → E_v (far field), ρ → ρ₀ (vacuum).
  - Near mass (E < E_v), ρ > ρ₀; for Γ=2, ρ ~ 1/(1 - k/r)^2 ≈ ρ₀ (1 + 2k/r) weakly—mimics DM density falloff ~1/r² (isothermal halos).
