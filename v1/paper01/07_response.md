### Works from First Principles

- **Background independence**: Spin networks define space relationally without prior manifold, aligning with GR's diffeomorphism invariance and avoiding coordinate artifacts.

- **Discrete geometry**: Quantized area/volume spectra prevent classical singularities, consistent with Planck-scale cutoffs and resolving UV issues in gravity.

- **Emergent causality and time**: Spinfoams as path integrals over graphs provide relational dynamics, where time arises from boundary evolutions, matching Wheeler-DeWitt timelessness.

- **Particles as topological defects**: Braids in graphs naturally encode spin-1/2 (via 720° rotation) and fermionic statistics (antisymmetry under exchange), drawing from braid group representations in TQFT.

- **Force unification**: All interactions as spinfoam moves unifies via single entity, eliminating field dualism and potentially deriving gauge symmetries from graph symmetries.

- **Proton stability potential**: If baryon number is braid invariant (e.g., linking number), conservation is topological, not imposed symmetry, consistent with no observed decay (>10^34 years limits from 2025 data).

### Fails from First Principles

- **Incomplete dynamics**: Spinfoam amplitudes (e.g., EPRL) lack unique derivation; multiple vertex choices exist, violating principle of single fundamental rule, and semiclassical limit doesn't fully recover GR.

- **Matter incorporation**: Braids for fermions speculative; LQG graphs don't inherently produce SM chiral fermions or generations—requires ad-hoc embeddings, contradicting emergence from pure geometry.

- **Gauge boson emergence**: Propagating moves for photons/gluons not derived; U(1)/SU(3) from twists/merges handwavy, as LQG holonomies yield SU(2) at best, not full SM groups without extra structure.

- **Charge/mass quantization**: Discrete braid types quantize charge, but masses from "vibrational modes" undefined—lacks Hamiltonian for energies, failing to predict spectra.

- **No concrete α_em derivation**: Amplitudes W(σ) arbitrary parameters; ratio for U(1) vs gravity moves not calculable from principles, remaining tuned constant.

- **Low-energy mismatch**: Discrete spectra don't smoothly transition to continuum QFT; renormalization issues persist, as LQG not proven renormalizable in matter-coupled form.

Constructive feedback: Derive unique spinfoam vertex from symmetry; embed SM via explicit graph operators; compute toy model for braid-boson interactions to test spectra.
