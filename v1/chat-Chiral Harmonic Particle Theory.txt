**IV. Gravity**

*   **A. Reinterpretation of Gravity:**
    *   Gravity is *not* a fundamental force or the curvature of space-time (as in GR).
    *   Instead, it is an emergent phenomenon resulting from the interaction of matter (knots) with the field.
*   **B. Field Consumption by Mass:**
    *   Mass (stable particle knots) "consumes" or incorporates the surrounding field into its structure.
    *   This consumption creates localized regions of high field density within the particle.
*   **C. Density Gradients and Motion:**
    *   The consumption of field by mass creates density gradients in the surrounding field: high density near/within the mass, lower density farther away.
    *   Objects (propagating knots) follow straight paths through the field.
    *   However, because the field itself is consumed/structured by mass, these "straight" paths appear curved or attracted towards the mass.
    *   This is the mechanism underlying gravitational attraction.
*   **D. Geodesic Motion:**
    *   The apparent curved paths of objects in gravitational fields are reinterpreted as straight-line motion through a field that has been locally consumed and restructured by mass.

**V. Cosmological Implications**

*   **A. Dark Matter:**
    *   Reinterpreted as a "missing field" effect, not missing matter.
    *   Massive objects consume field, creating steep density gradients.
    *   The surrounding unconstrained field tends to flow/dissipate away from this steep gradient.
    *   This creates an extended, diffuse region around massive bodies.
    *   The gravitational effects of this extended field gradient mimic the effects attributed to dark matter halos.
    *   The "missing mass" is the unaccounted-for influence of the field's geometric structure and dynamics.
*   **B. Dark Energy:**
    *   Linked to the natural expansive tendency of the unconstrained vacuum field.
    *   Regions of space with little matter (vacuum) have a larger effective field volume/density compared to regions with matter (which consumes field).
    *   The pressure differential between unconstrained (expansive) vacuum field and constrained (by matter) field drives cosmic acceleration.
    *   This explains dark energy without requiring a separate cosmological constant or exotic field.
*   **C. Redshift:**
    *   Cosmological and gravitational redshift are consequences of harmonic propagation through varying field densities.
    *   **Cosmological Redshift:** As light (a propagating harmonic) travels through expanding, dissipating vacuum regions, its waveform stretches, lowering frequency.
    *   **Gravitational Redshift:** Light propagating away from a massive body (high field consumption/density) experiences changes in the field medium, leading to a shift to lower energy/frequency.
*   **D. Large-Scale Structure:**
    *   The formation of cosmic structures (filaments, voids) could potentially be explained by the collective effects of field consumption (creating dense regions) and dissipation (creating voids), driven by the distribution of stable harmonic knots (matter).
