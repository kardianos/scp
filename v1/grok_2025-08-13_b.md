### 4. Implications for Dark Matter and Scalability
For galactic scales, if visible mass m_vis creates a trough, the halo mass m_halo ≈ m_vis (from the ratio above) could explain flat rotation curves without exotic particles, as v² / r ≈ G (m_vis + m_halo) / r² ≈ 2 G m_vis / r², yielding v ≈ constant for large r (if halo extends effectively). Non-linear solutions (solvable numerically via ODE for radial E) might yield Γ-dependent ratios, e.g., m_halo / m_vis ∝ Γ for stronger compression.
