# Sourced Maxwell Equations from Soliton Topology

## Goal

Derive the sourced Maxwell equation ∇**F** = J from the HFKT field equation, where J is the electromagnetic current generated by a topological soliton. This requires two sub-results: (a) deriving electric charge Q_EM from the topological baryon number Q via isospin orientation, and (b) showing that the bivector perturbation **F** around a soliton background satisfies an inhomogeneous wave equation sourced by the soliton's topological structure.

After this task we will know whether the theory's free Maxwell equations (established: □**F** = 0, FOUNDATION.md §15) extend to sourced Maxwell equations with the correct charge structure, or whether an additional coupling mechanism is required.

## Technical Requirements

### Sub-task A: Isospin current and charge identification

**Mathematical derivation (pen-and-paper, then verify numerically):**

In the Skyrme model, electromagnetic charge comes from gauging U(1)_EM ⊂ SU(2)_isospin. The standard result (Adkins, Nappi, Witten 1983) is:

```
Q_EM = (B + I₃) / 2
```

where B is baryon number and I₃ is the third component of isospin. For the hedgehog q₀ = ρ₀(cos f + sin f r̂·σ), the isospin current is:

```
J^μ_EM = ∂L/∂A_μ |_{A=0}
```

where A_μ is the U(1)_EM gauge field and the minimal coupling is ∂_μq → D_μq = ∂_μq - ieA_μ[τ₃/2, q].

**What must be derived:**

1. The explicit form of D_μq in terms of our Cl⁺(3,0,1) components (s, f1, f2, f3). The generator τ₃/2 acts as left-multiplication by (1/2)e₁₂ in our basis.
2. The variation δL₂/δA_μ and δL₄/δA_μ evaluated at A_μ = 0, yielding J^μ_EM as a function of f(r), f'(r), and angular coordinates.
3. Verification that ∫J⁰ d³x = Q_EM = (B + I₃)/2.

**What code is needed:**

- Profile loader (copy from v2)
- Numerical integration of J⁰ over the soliton volume
- Angular integration for the isospin current (Gauss-Legendre on S², copy quadrature from v2)

### Sub-task B: Linearization of the EOM around soliton background

**Mathematical derivation:**

Write Ψ = Ψ₀ + εδΨ where Ψ₀ is the static hedgehog and δΨ is a perturbation with spatial bivector character (δΨ.f1, δΨ.f2, δΨ.f3 nonzero; other components zero or secondary).

Expand the full EOM (FOUNDATION.md §8) to linear order in ε:

```
(1/c²)∂²_t(δΨ) - ∇²(δΨ) + [δ(L₄ force)/δΨ]_{Ψ₀} · δΨ + λ[...] + μ²δp = 0
```

The key computation is the Hessian of L₄:

```
H₄[δΨ] = d/dε [δL₄/δΨ (Ψ₀ + εδΨ)] |_{ε=0}
```

This is a position-dependent linear operator acting on δΨ. For a bivector perturbation δF propagating on the hedgehog background, this gives a coupled system:

```
□(δF) + M²(r, θ) · δF = J_source(r, θ)
```

where M²(r,θ) encodes the effective mass/coupling from the soliton background and J_source is the inhomogeneous driving term.

**What must be derived:**

1. The explicit form of the L₄ Hessian for the hedgehog background. This involves second variations of the Skyrme G-tensor.
2. The decomposition of the coupled bivector equation into angular momentum channels (spherical harmonics on the hedgehog background).
3. The identification of J_source with the electromagnetic current from Sub-task A.

**What code is needed:**

- Hedgehog background initialization on 3D grid (copy from v2 verify3d.c)
- Hessian computation: finite-difference verification of the analytical H₄ (perturb Ψ₀ → Ψ₀ + εδF, measure force change, compare with H₄·δF)
- Radial PDE solver for the coupled channel equations (adapt from v2 normal_modes.c Sturm-Liouville infrastructure)

### Sub-task C: Electromagnetic form factors

Once J^μ_EM is known on the soliton background:

2. Compute the magnetic form factor from the spatial current J^i
3. Extract the proton magnetic moment μ_p and charge radius r_E

**What code is needed:**

- Numerical Fourier transform of the current density

## Success Criteria

1. **Charge quantization**: ∫J⁰ d³x = Q_EM = 1.000 ± 0.001 for the proton (B=1, I₃=+1/2) orientation. Q_EM = 0.000 ± 0.001 for the neutron (B=1, I₃=-1/2) orientation.

2. **Current conservation**: ∂_μ J^μ = 0 verified numerically to at least 4 significant figures on the hedgehog profile.

3. **Far-field Coulomb**: Outside the soliton core (r > 3 code units), E(r) = Q_EM/(4πr²) to within 0.1% relative error. The electrostatic potential φ(r) → Q_EM/(4πr) at large r.

4. **Linearized sourcing**: The bivector perturbation equation □(δF) + M²·δF = J reduces in the far field (M² → 0 as r → ∞) to the standard sourced Maxwell equation with J matching the current from Sub-task A.

5. **Hessian verification**: The analytical L₄ Hessian matches the finite-difference Hessian to 4th-order convergence in the perturbation amplitude ε (i.e., the error scales as ε² for central differences).

6. **Form factors** (stretch goal): Charge radius r_E within 30% of experimental (0.84 fm). Magnetic moment μ_p within 50% of experimental (2.79 n.m.). These are known to be approximate in the Skyrme model — matching Adkins-Nappi-Witten (1983) values is the real target: r_E ≈ 0.59 fm, μ_p ≈ 2.34 n.m. at their parameter choice.

---

## Null Result Criteria

1. **Charge not topologically determined**: If Q_EM depends on a continuous parameter (isospin orientation angle) that is not fixed by the field equations, then electric charge is NOT a topological invariant — it requires collective coordinate quantization (as in standard Skyrme). This is not a failure of the theory but changes the interpretation: charge quantization is quantum-mechanical, not classical-topological. **Record the explicit dependence Q_EM(α) where α is the collective coordinate.**

2. **Bivector does not couple to soliton**: If the Hessian M²(r) vanishes identically for bivector perturbations (i.e., the linearized equation is just □δF = 0 with no source), then the soliton does NOT generate EM fields at the classical level. This would mean sourced Maxwell requires either: (a) quantum corrections (collective coordinate quantization → Noether current), or (b) explicit gauging that goes beyond the ungauged Lagrangian. **This would be a significant structural result**: it would mean the free Maxwell equations (§15) are exact even in the presence of solitons, and all EM sourcing is a quantum effect.

3. **Current not conserved**: If ∂_μJ^μ ≠ 0 for any proposed current, the identification is wrong. Try alternative current definitions before declaring null.

4. **Form factors wildly wrong**: If r_E or μ_p differ from Adkins-Nappi-Witten by more than a factor of 3, there may be a sign error or a fundamental problem with the U(1) embedding. Check against the Skyrme literature before declaring null.
